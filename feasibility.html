Project Overview

The College Project is a £40 million campus modernisation programme comprising 12 new facilities: undergraduate and postgraduate accommodation, a sports pavilion, an assembly hall, a visiting fellow's flat, and landscaped grounds. The development is funded by a £40M bond at 4.5% annual interest, repayable over 45 years.

This study was prepared at RIBA Stage 1 — Preparation and Briefing, the critical early stage at which risk management foundations are established before design commitments are made. The work produced two primary deliverables: a comprehensive Order of Cost Estimate (OCE) and cash flow model, and an ISO 31000-aligned Risk Management Plan covering all risk categories from financial to societal and environmental.

The cash flow model runs from January 2024 through 2069 — spanning construction (35 months), operational ramp-up, and full steady-state income — modelling 7 separate rental income streams alongside bond financing, maintenance costs, and inflation-adjusted construction spend. The NPV analysis discounted at 5% returns a positive net present value of £8.5M, confirming project viability across all scenarios.

Across the risk register, two critical risks scored 9/9 (Maximum severity): material cost inflation and phased construction challenges — both with active mitigation strategies, named risk owners, and escalation procedures defined at RIBA Stage 1.

Risk Register & Management Framework

A 17-risk register was developed using ISO 31000:2018 and RICS practice standards, structured across 7 Risk Breakdown Structure (RBS) categories. Each risk was scored using a 3×3 Likelihood × Impact matrix. Two risks scored 9/9 — the maximum severity level — requiring immediate and proactive management with dedicated risk owners and fallback plans.

Risk Management Framework — 6 Strategies

Strategy 01
Avoidance
Eliminating risks by changing plans. Regulatory delays avoided via early permit submission. Unstable ground conditions avoided via pre-construction geotechnical surveys.
Strategy 02
Mitigation
Reducing impact through proactive measures. Material cost inflation mitigated via long-term fixed-price supplier contracts. Phased construction managed through precise scheduling and contractor collaboration.
Strategy 03
Transfer
Shifting risk to another party. Interest rate risk transferred via fixed-rate bond financing. H&S accident risk transferred through comprehensive insurance and third-party liability coverage.
Strategy 04
Sharing
Distributing risk across parties. Supply chain risk shared by engaging multiple suppliers. Sustainability integration risks shared with specialist consultants for solar panels and heat pump systems.
Strategy 05
Retention
Accepting manageable risks within contingency. Community opposition accepted with monitoring. Minor construction defects retained within the project contingency budget.
Strategy 06
Control & Review
Ongoing oversight and reassessment. Real-time monitoring via PM software. Regular coordination meetings. Continuous scope change review with College stakeholders.
