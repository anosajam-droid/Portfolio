Project Context

Astria Mixed Development is a high-density mixed-use scheme on John Garne Way, Marston, Oxford — spanning 10 acres with a 20-storey residential tower, a 5-storey commercial building, luxury apartments, and recreational amenities. The 24-month project is commissioned by Horizon Developers.

This BIM Execution Plan was prepared at post-appointment stage under ISO 19650-2:2018 — the critical point at which the delivery team is confirmed and information management responsibilities must be formally established before any modelling begins. The BEP serves as the binding reference document for all digital delivery throughout the project lifecycle, reviewed and updated at every milestone.

The plan follows the four-step BIM Execution Planning Procedure: Identify BIM Uses and Goals → Define Project Execution Process → Develop Information Exchanges → Define Supporting Infrastructure.

Standards & Compliance

ISO 19650 Series
Information Management
Collaborative production of architectural, engineering and construction information. Organisation and digitisation of information about buildings and civil engineering works including BIM.
CEN/EN 17412-1 · ISO 7817
Level of Information Need
Concepts and principles for defining the level of information need. Ensures information is requested and delivered at the right detail level at each project stage.
USIBD LOA · BS 5606
Accuracy & Tolerance
When capturing existing asset information, geometry must account for construction tolerances — ensuring spatial accuracy of federated models and preventing coordination failures.
ISO 4157-1 / ISO 4157-2
Component & Space Naming
Convention for room names, numbers, and component naming applied consistently across all deliverables. Supports space management, FM handover, and asset register compilation.
ISO 7200
Document Management
All exchanged information containers comply with ISO 7200 by including mandatory data fields in title blocks and document headers — ensuring traceability and version control throughout delivery.
ISO 55000 Series
Asset Management
Guidance on managing assets applied to the operate phase — ensuring BIM data captured during construction supports lifecycle asset management from handover onwards.
BS 8210
Facilities Maintenance
Detailed guidance on facilities maintenance management. Ensures BIM data captured during construction is formatted to support FM requirements post-handover.
BS 8536
Briefing for Design & Construction
Recommendations ensuring design takes account of expected asset performance over its planned operational life — connecting client brief to BIM information requirements.
Uniclass · Omniclass · Revit Categories
Classification
Classification system used for categorising scope of work and model deliverables — ensuring consistent naming and grouping of elements across all disciplines.
